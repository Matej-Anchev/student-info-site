4 Years

Bachelor of Mathematics

The first cycle provides foundational knowledge in pure and applied mathematics. Students develop strong analytical skills and are prepared for advanced studies or entry into professional roles.

Key Features:

  • Core mathematics courses (calculus, linear algebra, discrete mathematics)
  • Specialized courses in geometry, analysis, and topology
  • Applied mathematics and computational methods
  • Practical training and seminar work
  • Thesis option for advanced students

Important Information

  • Exam Sessions: January-February, June, August-September, and special March session
  • Class Schedules: Available at the start of each semester
  • Enrollment: Competitive entrance exam required
  • Contact: First Cycle Coordinator at the Institute

Second Cycle Studies

Master's Degree

2 Years

Master's in Mathematics

The second cycle offers advanced study in specialized areas of mathematics. Students conduct original research under faculty supervision and prepare for doctoral studies or specialized careers.

Specialization Areas:

  • Pure Mathematics (Algebra, Geometry, Analysis, Topology)
  • Applied Mathematics (Computational methods, Optimization)
  • Mathematical Research (Independent thesis work)

Program Structure

  • Duration: 2 academic years (4 semesters)
  • Coursework: Specialized seminars and advanced topics
  • Thesis: Original research under advisor supervision
  • Exams: Comprehensive oral defense
  • Degree: Master of Science in Mathematics

Third Cycle Studies

Doctoral Degree

3-4 Years

Ph.D. in Mathematics

The third cycle is dedicated to advanced research and the production of new mathematical knowledge. Doctoral candidates work closely with experienced researchers and contribute to the international mathematical community.

Research Areas:

  • Theoretical Mathematics: Algebra, Geometry, Analysis, Topology
  • Applied Mathematics: Computational Mathematics, Mathematical Modeling
  • Interdisciplinary Mathematics: Mathematical Physics, Mathematical Biology

Program Overview

  • Duration: 3-4 years (6-8 semesters)
  • Research Focus: Original dissertation work
  • Advanced Seminars: Specialized topics in research area
  • Final Examination: Public defense of dissertation
  • Degree: Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Mathematics

International Exchange & Mobility

Study Abroad Opportunities

The Institute participates in various international exchange programs including Erasmus+ and bilateral agreements with universities across Europe. Students at all levels can apply for semester or year-long exchanges.

Benefits:
